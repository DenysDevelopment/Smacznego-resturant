# PRODUCT.md — Smacznego

**Register:** brand (public surfaces: home, menu) with product discipline for the ordering flow (cart, checkout, order status).

## Product purpose

Online ordering for a single real neighbourhood restaurant in Poland serving
Ukrainian/Polish **home cooking** — barszcz (borscht), pierogi/vareniki, cutlets,
comfort food made for real. The public pages must make people hungry and trust the
kitchen; the ordering flow must be frictionless and honest (pay on delivery).

## Users

Hungry locals in a Polish city ordering dinner, mostly on their phones. Trilingual
audience: Polish (default), Ukrainian, Russian. They want to see the food, know it's
real and generous, and order in under a minute. Not foodies chasing a tasting menu —
people who want a good, hearty meal brought to the door.

## Brand voice — three physical words

**Warm · hearty · honest.** A generous ladle of borscht, a hand-folded pierogi, a
grandmother who doesn't fuss but feeds you well. Confident and appetite-first, never
fine-dining-precious and never fast-food-shouty. A neighbourhood canteen with soul.

## Anti-references (do NOT drift here)

- The **dark-moody-gourmet** reflex: near-black background, gold accent, Playfair/Cormorant serif, candle-lit "fine dining". This is the #1 training-data cliché for restaurants and we are actively leaving it.
- The **editorial-magazine** lane: display-serif italic + tiny tracked mono labels + ruled columns. Not our voice.
- Generic SaaS-cream + one-timid-accent restraint. Too quiet for a food brand.
- Emoji anywhere in the UI (SVG icons only).
- Colored-block / gradient placeholders where food photos belong — a bug, not restraint. Real photography is mandatory.
- Templated centered-stack: hero icon → title → subtitle → identical cards.

## Strategic principles

1. **Real food photography is non-negotiable.** Big, honest, appetizing images carry the brand. No solid rectangles standing in for a dish.
2. **Committed color rooted in the food** — the palette comes from the plate (beetroot, paprika, sour-cream, rye), not from a "gourmet" convention.
3. **Distinctive, Cyrillic-capable type.** No reflex fonts (no Playfair/Cormorant/Fraunces/Inter). Must render pl + uk + ru beautifully.
4. **Ordering is frictionless.** Cart, options, checkout: fast, legible, obviously trustworthy for pay-on-delivery.
5. **Mobile-first**, because that's where dinner gets ordered.
